I'm a Mechatronics and Robotics Engineering student at Queen's University with a strong interest in biomedical engineering and designing systems that directly impact healthcare. I'm passionate about blending robotics and human-centered design to create accessible, user-friendly solutions that improve lives.

My hands-on experience includes working with KINARM robotic systems at the LIMB Lab, where I focused on improving testing protocols and supporting clinical diagnostics. I developed and refined testing programs using Simulink, managed data integrity in REDCap, and collaborated with healthcare professionals during data collection, deepening my understanding of assistive technologies.

A pivotal moment in my third year was joining Queen's AutoDrive, where I worked in both the embedded systems and operations divisions. In the SAE GM AutoDrive Mobility Innovation Challenge 2024, I led a team of four to design a level 5 autonomous car aimed at assisting people with disabilities, particularly focusing on individuals with cerebral palsy.
